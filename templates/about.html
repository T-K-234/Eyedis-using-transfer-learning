About the Project

Our project focuses on leveraging artificial intelligence to improve the early detection and classification of eye diseases. By utilizing advanced deep learning techniques, we aim to assist medical professionals in providing timely and accurate diagnoses, ultimately improving patient outcomes.

Ensemble Learning Model

This system combines the strengths of multiple models to enhance diagnostic accuracy and reliability in eye disease classification. By aggregating predictions from different architectures, the ensemble model reduces the risk of misclassification and improves robustness.

Convolutional Neural Network (CNN)

The CNN model processes RGB eye images and captures spatial patterns. It is particularly effective in detecting texture- and shape-related features associated with eye diseases.

ResNet-18

ResNet-18 uses residual connections to enable efficient training of deep networks by mitigating the vanishing gradient problem, resulting in robust and accurate image classification.

Swin Transformer

The Swin Transformer offers a hierarchical vision transformer structure that processes images in non-overlapping windows. It provides high performance in classifying localized disease regions in eye images while being computationally efficient.
